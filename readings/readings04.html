Optional Reading

I higly recommend reading Banzhaf (2009). Here is a stable URL to the pdf. Benefit-cost analysis (BCA) is a requirement for many public resource allocation decisions. This history of the intellectual debate and political considerations between two visions for BCA in the U.S. – multi-objective and single-objective analysis – is illuminating. You will learn about theoretical and practical strengths/limitations of each approach and how that relates to different views of the role of the scientist/analyst in conducting a decision analysis to inform decisions. In addition, because BCA is a prevailing paradigm, you should be familiar with its conceptual underpinnings and assumptions.

I also recommend reading the papers below to get exposure to papers that focus on trade-offs and synergies as key framing points of the decision analysis.

  • Pollack et al. (2025)
  • Jafino et al. (2022)
  • Ciullo et al. (2020)
  • Hadka et al. (2015)
